# 1. Concept Overview: GRA and Obnulënka

The GRA (Goal–Reality–Agent) framework is a meta‑architecture for modeling and evolving **structures** (frames, ontologies, theories). At its core is the **obnulënka** (reset) mechanism: a continuous process of evaluating structures against reality and regenerating them.

## Key Components

- **GRAFrame**: An abstract representation of a class of models or laws. It has two essential methods:
  - `instantiate(params)`: creates a concrete instance (e.g., a specific function, a cosmological model with given parameters).
  - `score(data)`: returns a measure of how well the instantiated model fits the data (e.g., log‑likelihood, χ², or a custom consistency metric).

- **GRAResetController**: Manages a population of frames. At each step:
  1. Evaluates all frames on the current data (or a batch).
  2. Applies a **reset policy** to decide which frames survive and which are killed.
  3. Replaces killed frames with new ones generated by mutating surviving frames.

- **GRAAgent**: A generator of new frames. It can apply mutation operators (e.g., tweak parameters, change functional form, combine two frames) to produce offspring.

## The Reset Cycle

1. **Initialisation**: Start with a diverse set of frames (some naive, some informed).
2. **Score**: For each frame, instantiate it with some parameters and evaluate against data.
3. **Select/Kill**: Based on scores, keep top performers; remove the rest.
4. **Mutate**: Use agents to create variations of the survivors.
5. **Repeat**: Go to step 2 until convergence or a stop condition.

This is reminiscent of evolutionary algorithms, but the key difference is that the objects of evolution are **structures themselves** – the frames – not just parameters. Frames can represent completely different ontologies or laws, and the reset process can discover entirely new classes of models.

## Why "Obnulënka"?

The Russian word "обнуление" means "setting to zero" or "resetting". In GRA, underperforming frames are **zeroed out** – removed from the population – making room for new structural hypotheses. The process is a constant **reset** of the hypothesis space towards better alignment with reality.
